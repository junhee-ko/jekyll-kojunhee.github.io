<p style="margin: 0px; text-indent: 18px; font-size: 14px; font-family: Cochin;">
  The materials to build your Jumbo Tower (I‚Äôll use that name to avoid any legal issues) is very simple. You need to go to your local building supplier and pick up 5 2x3x8 studs. <p style="margin: 0px; text-align: center; font-size: 18px; font-family: Cochin;">
  <strong>TOOLS</strong>
</p>

<p style="margin: 0px; text-indent: 18px; font-size: 14px; font-family: Cochin;">
  The bare minimum tools that you need for this project are a saw, and some sand paper. A good hand saw, and a sanding block will set you back around 20 dollars. You will however spend a couple days sanding.
</p>

<img class="alignleft" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; border: 0px none;" title="saw.png" alt="saw" src="http://www.colinmcnamara.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/saw.png" width="172" height="226" border="0" /><img style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="sanpdpaper.png" alt="sanpdpaper" src="http://www.colinmcnamara.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/sanpdpaper.png" width="177" height="223" border="0" />

<p style="margin: 0px; text-indent: 18px; font-size: 14px; font-family: Cochin;">
  I chose however to use a Sliding Compound Miter Saw, and a Belt sander.¬† You can do perfectly find with a hand held circular saw (30 bucks or so at Harbor Freight). I would however recommend getting the belt sander. It saves LOTS OF TIME.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0px; text-align: center; font-size: 18px; font-family: Cochin;">
  <strong>Process</strong>
</p>

<p style="margin: 0px; text-indent: 18px; font-size: 14px; font-family: Cochin;">
  <strong>Step 1¬† &#8211;</strong> Have you kids get the lumber out of the truck. They need to learn what hard work is.
</p>

<img style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="child-labor.png" alt="child-labor" src="http://www.colinmcnamara.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/child-labor.png" width="600" height="495" border="0" />

<p style="margin: 0px; text-indent: 18px; font-size: 14px; font-family: Cochin;">
  <strong>Step 2 &#8211;</strong> Have you kids sand down all four sides of the 2x3x8 studs. Remember to round the edges a bit too.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0px; font-size: 12px;">
  <img style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="topher-belt-sander.png" alt="topher belt sander" src="http://www.colinmcnamara.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/topher-belt-sander.png" width="500" height="437" border="0" />
</p>

<p style="margin: 0px; text-indent: 18px; font-size: 14px; font-family: Cochin;">
  <strong>Step 3 &#8211;</strong> Cut the blocks into 7.5‚Äù pieces. I used a Compound Mitre Saw with block clamped on so I could burn through the lumber. I didn‚Äôt make Chris do this step, though I walked him through a couple cuts so he learns tool safety.
</p>

<img style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="mitre-saw.png" alt="mitre saw" src="http://www.colinmcnamara.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/mitre-saw.png" width="480" height="480" border="0" />

<p style="margin: 0px; text-indent: 18px; font-size: 14px; font-family: Cochin;">
  <strong>Step 4-</strong> Make a bench sander &#8211;¬† I don‚Äôt have a bench sander. I do however have a wealth of redneck ingenuity. With that gift, my hand held belt sander, and couple clamps I made a redneck bench sander.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0px; font-size: 12px;">
  <img style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="redneck-bench-sander.png" alt="redneck bench sander" src="http://www.colinmcnamara.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/redneck-bench-sander.png" width="500" height="575" border="0" />
</p>

<p style="margin: 0px; text-indent: 18px; font-size: 14px; font-family: Cochin;">
  <strong>Step 5 &#8211;</strong> Sand the ends, and round the edges of your Jumbo Tower blocks. The easiest way is to sand the cut ends first, then lay each side pretty flat against belt while lifting the end up. This will put a nice smooth edge on each side, with some sharp corners. Roll each corner against the belt to smooth it out and you have a nice sanded lock.
</p>
